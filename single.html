Hands down the best chimichurri by far

August 19,2020
1

I feel that it's important for me to state in my first post that I have no intention to diss businesses and their creations. I may sound biased at times, but I will do my best to be as fair as possible.


Ok, without any further ado, let's get started.



The reason I picked LALA'S Argentine Grill as my first post is because I LOVE their chimicurri, and it's what inspired me to start this blog. I've tried other chimichurri at other establishments, but nothing beats first love.(...RIGHT?!)
Now, a little bit about this restaurant.
They opened their first location on Melrose avenue back in 1996. LALA'S is a landmark on Melrose amongst other old restaurants like Frankie's and Antonio's.

We came to California with a commitment to our Argentinian culture, a profound appreciation for the United States, and a desire to create something that fused the best of both worlds.


Prior to visiting LALA'S for the first time this year, I'd never tried or even heard of chimichurri. But now it's all I see and think about. It's safe to say it's an addiction. They serve you a basket of bread and chimichurri the moment you are seated. My first reaction after the first bite?
"WHAT IS THIS!"
My friend and I were NOT ashamed to ask our server for more chimichurri. We gotta do what we gotta do. I can also talk about their food, but this blog is about chimichurri, so let's stick with it.


Upon starting this blog, I had to do some research, and ask Google Sensei about chimichurri.
Ovbiously, one of the search results was a chimichurri wikipedia page.
Wait, what?
International Chimichurri Day? Created by LALA'S?? And it's happening in 2 months?!

Let's talk about their chimichurri now. I like vinegary food. So it makes sense I love their chimichurri. It has some red crushed peppers, garlic and oregano, probably just like any other chimichurri. Before I left the restaurant, I found out that they sell it by the jar...so I got 2 of them.. I saw on their Instagram that people are using it on tacos and dumplings. I guess I have a lot of experimenting to do with this!
